<p><p>The diamond semiconductor substrates market is divided into two primary types: natural and synthetic. Natural diamond substrates are sourced from mining and offer unique properties, including high thermal conductivity and excellent electrical insulation, but they are limited in availability and can be expensive. Conversely, synthetic diamond substrates are manufactured using techniques like chemical vapor deposition (CVD), allowing for greater control over quality and size. They are increasingly favored for their consistency and cost-effectiveness, making them ideal for various semiconductor applications.</p></p>
