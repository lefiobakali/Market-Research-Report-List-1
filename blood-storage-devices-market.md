<p><p>The blood storage devices market includes refrigerators and freezers specifically designed for preserving blood and blood products. Refrigerators maintain temperatures between 1°C to 6°C, ideal for storing red blood cells and plasma short-term. Freezers, on the other hand, operate at temperatures below -18°C, ensuring long-term preservation of components like platelets and cryoprecipitates. Both devices are crucial in hospitals and blood banks, maintaining the integrity and viability of blood for transfusions and medical procedures, thereby enhancing patient care.</p></p>
