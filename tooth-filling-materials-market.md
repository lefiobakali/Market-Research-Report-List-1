<p><p>The tooth filling materials market comprises various types tailored for restoring dental cavities. Glass ionomer, which bonds chemically with enamel, is ideal for patients with minimal wear. Silver amalgam is durable and cost-effective, commonly used in posterior teeth. Composite fillings offer aesthetic appeal, blending well with natural teeth. Ceramic fillings are resilient and less abrasive, while gold fillings are long-lasting but expensive. Other materials include resin and temporary fillings, catering to diverse patient needs and preferences in dental care.</p></p>
