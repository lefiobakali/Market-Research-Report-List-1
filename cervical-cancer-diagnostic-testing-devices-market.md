<p><p>The cervical cancer diagnostic testing devices market is primarily divided into two main types: cytopathological methods and molecular methods. Cytopathological methods involve examining cells from the cervix for abnormalities, typically using Pap tests or liquid-based cytology. Molecular methods focus on detecting specific genetic material or biomarkers associated with cervical cancer, such as HPV DNA testing. Both approaches are crucial for early detection and diagnosis, enabling timely intervention and improving patient outcomes in cervical cancer management.</p></p>
