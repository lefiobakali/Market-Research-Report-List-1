<p><p>Active infrared sensors are devices that emit infrared light and measure the reflected light to detect objects or measure distances. These sensors comprise a light source, typically an infrared LED or laser, and a photodetector. When the emitted light hits an object, it reflects back to the sensor, allowing for object detection, distance measurement, or motion detection. <p><p>The active infrared sensors market is primarily categorized into two types: thermal and quantum sensors. Thermal infrared sensors detect radiation emitted by objects based on their temperature, making them ideal for applications like surveillance, firefighting, and industrial monitoring. Quantum infrared sensors, on the other hand, utilize materials that respond to changes in infrared wavelengths, offering higher sensitivity and precision. These sensors are widely used in defense, telecommunications, and environmental monitoring due to their ability to detect specific wavelengths and improve accuracy.</p></p>
