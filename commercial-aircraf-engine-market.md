<p><p>The commercial aircraft engine market comprises several types: turboprop, turbofan, turboshaft, and piston engines. Turboprop engines combine a turbine and propeller, ideal for short-haul flights. Turbofan engines, prevalent in larger aircraft, offer higher efficiency at cruising speeds with quieter operations. Turboshaft engines power helicopters, delivering high power-to-weight ratios for vertical flight. Piston engines, typically found in small general aviation aircraft, operate on a reciprocating mechanism, making them simple and cost-effective for light aircraft operations.</p></p>
